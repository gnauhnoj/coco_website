About

What We're Trying to Achieve

The visually impaired “see” by utilizing their other senses. By using touch to gather the small details of a scene, they are able to cognitively create an accurate reconstruction of a situation even without “seeing.” How can we apply these concepts to the web? The goal of palpiction is to examine whether it's possible to accurately reconstruct digital images based solely on touch and the objects that are present within an image.

palpiction presents users a series of "blinded" images which users touch to explore. As object are discovered in the image, palpiction reads out the object's identity through text-to-speech. Users are tested on their understanding of the image and accumulate points based on performance. For more information, visit our blog.

This application takes advantage of the Microsoft COCO dataset. The associated terms of use apply.
